The Evolution of Financial Advice

1

Full Dependence

Before the internet, investors relied entirely on brokers for information and transactions. Stock quotes required a phone call or office visit, and most advice was tied to commission-based product sales.

2

Commission Compression

With technology and online trading, commissions dropped, and self-directed platforms emerged. Robinhood led the charge to zero-commission trading, now standard across most brokerages.

3

Rise of the AUM Model

As commissions declined, many advisors shifted to charging based on assets under management (AUM), regardless of their contribution to a client’s overall financial outcome.

4

Technology Expands Access

Cloud-based planning tools, aggregation platforms, and automated analysis allow advisors to serve more people—including those not considered HNW (>$1M) or UHNW (>$30M) by traditional firms.

5

Better Products for All

Low-cost ETFs, cash sweep networks, and FDIC-maximizing solutions give everyday investors access to tools once reserved for institutions or high-net-worth individuals.

6

Virtual Becomes the Norm

The COVID-19 pandemic normalized virtual meetings, increasing convenience and decreasing costs. Financial planning became more scalable, flexible, and efficient.

7

The DIWY Model

Do-It-With-You financial planning blends the autonomy of DIY with the support of a fiduciary advisor. You keep control and custody while receiving guidance on your terms—flat-fee, flexible, and collaborative.
